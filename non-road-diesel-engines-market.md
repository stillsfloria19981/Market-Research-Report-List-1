<p><p>The non-road diesel engines market is segmented into three primary types based on cooling methods: water-cooled, air-cooled, and oil-cooled engines. Water-cooled engines utilize a liquid cooling system for efficient thermal management, making them ideal for heavy-duty applications. Air-cooled engines rely on ambient air to dissipate heat, favoring lightweight and compact designs for portable equipment. Oil-cooled engines circulate oil to regulate temperature, offering reliability and durability in various conditions. Each type is tailored to specific operational needs and environmental conditions.</p></p>
