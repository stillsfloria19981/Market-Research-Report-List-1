<p><p>The egg allergy therapeutics market is primarily divided into three types. Epinephrine injections are vital for treating severe allergic reactions, providing immediate relief by constricting blood vessels and opening airways. Antihistamines are used for milder reactions, alleviating symptoms such as hives and itching by blocking histamine release. Other market options include corticosteroids and immunotherapy treatments, which aim to reduce overall sensitivity to egg allergens over time. Together, these therapies cater to varying degrees of egg allergy severity and patient needs.</p></p>
