<p><p>Cathode active materials for lithium-ion batteries are crucial for energy storage performance. Lithium Cobalt Oxide (LCO) offers high energy density but limited lifespan. Lithium Manganese Oxide (LMO) is known for thermal stability and enhanced safety. Lithium Iron Phosphate (LFP) provides excellent thermal stability and longevity but has lower energy density. Lithium Nickel Cobalt Manganese Oxide (NMC) balances energy density, cost, and safety, while Lithium Nickel Cobalt Aluminum Oxide (NCA) excels in energy capacity and performance, particularly in electric vehicles.</p></p>
