<p><p>E-cigarettes, or electronic cigarettes, are battery-operated devices that heat a liquid (e-liquid or vape juice) to produce an aerosol, commonly referred to as vapor, which is inhaled by the user. Vaping refers to the act of using these devices. E-cigarettes are often marketed as a less harmful alternative to traditional tobacco smoking, as they do not involve combustion and typically contain fewer toxic chemicals. However, concerns remain about their long-term health effects and their appeal to younger demographics.</p><p>The E-cigarette and Vaping Market has experienced significant growth in recent years, driven by increasing consumer demand for alternatives to traditional smoking, technological advancements in vaping devices, and the availability of a wide range of flavors. The market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 5.7% during the forecast period. Key trends include the rise of disposable e-cigarettes, the development of nicotine salts for smoother vaping experiences, and the growing popularity of pod-based systems. Additionally, regulatory changes and public health campaigns are shaping the market landscape, with some regions imposing stricter controls on advertising and sales to minors. Despite these challenges, the market continues to expand, particularly in North America, Europe, and Asia-Pacific, as more consumers adopt vaping as a lifestyle choice or smoking cessation tool.</p></p>
