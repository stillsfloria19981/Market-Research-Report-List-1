<p><p>Isostatic pressing is a method used to apply pressure uniformly on materials to enhance their density and structural integrity, with two primary market types: wet bag pressing and dry bag pressing. Wet bag pressing utilizes a liquid medium to transmit pressure, allowing for better compaction of complex shapes and materials. In contrast, dry bag pressing employs a solid medium, resulting in quicker processing times and lower costs, making it suitable for simpler geometries. Both methods cater to diverse industrial applications, including ceramics and metals.</p></p>
