<p><p>A two-wheeler catalytic converter is an emission control device installed in motorcycles and scooters to reduce harmful pollutants like carbon monoxide, hydrocarbons, and nitrogen oxides from exhaust gases. It converts these pollutants into less harmful substances such as carbon dioxide, water vapor, and nitrogen through chemical reactions facilitated by precious metals like platinum, palladium, and rhodium. With increasing environmental regulations and growing awareness of air pollution, the demand for catalytic converters in two-wheelers has risen significantly.</p><p>The Two-Wheeler Catalytic Converter Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 5.5% during the forecast period. Additionally, the rising adoption of electric two-wheelers and hybrid models is influencing market dynamics, pushing manufacturers to innovate and improve catalytic converter efficiency.</p><p>Key trends in the market include the development of lightweight and cost-effective catalytic converters, integration of advanced materials for better performance, and the expansion of production facilities in emerging markets. <p><p>The two-wheeler catalytic converter market includes technologies like Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR), Lean NOx Catalysts (LNC), Lean NOx Trap (LNT), and others. SCR reduces nitrogen oxides (NOx) using a urea-based solution. LNC utilizes catalysts to convert NOx into nitrogen and oxygen in lean-burn conditions. LNT temporarily stores NOx and reduces it during periodic rich-burn phases. Other technologies focus on alternative methods for emission control. These systems aim to minimize harmful emissions, comply with regulations, and enhance environmental sustainability in two-wheelers.</p></p>
