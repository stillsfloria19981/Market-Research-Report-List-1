<p><p>The bus aluminum alloy wheel market includes three main types: casting, forging, and others. Casting involves pouring molten aluminum into molds, creating complex shapes with good surface finish but lower strength. Forging involves shaping solid aluminum under high pressure, resulting in stronger and more durable wheels suitable for heavy loads. Other methods may include machining and extrusion, which offer alternative ways to produce wheel designs meeting specific performance requirements. Each type caters to different operational needs and cost considerations in the bus industry.</p></p>
