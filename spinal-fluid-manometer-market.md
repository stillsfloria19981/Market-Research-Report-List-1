<p><p>A Spinal Fluid Manometer is a medical device used to measure the pressure of cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) in the spinal canal and ventricles of the brain. It is crucial for diagnosing conditions such as increased intracranial pressure, hydrocephalus, and other neurological disorders. The manometer typically consists of a connected tube filled with a fluid that rises or falls in response to changes in CSF pressure, providing clinicians with essential information about the patient's health.</p><p>The Spinal Fluid Manometer Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 7.3% during the forecast period, driven by factors such as increasing prevalence of neurological diseases, advancements in medical technologies, and an aging population that is more susceptible to these conditions. Furthermore, growing awareness about the importance of early diagnosis and treatment of CNS disorders is fueling demand for effective diagnostic tools. </p><p>Latest trends in the market include the development of innovative manometer designs for enhanced accuracy and ease of use, as well as the integration of digital technologies to facilitate real-time monitoring.
