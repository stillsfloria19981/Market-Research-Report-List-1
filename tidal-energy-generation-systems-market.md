<p><p>Tidal energy generation systems can be categorized into three primary types: coastal, river, and others. Coastal systems harness the energy from ocean tides near shorelines, utilizing structures like tidal barrages to convert kinetic energy into electricity. River systems, on the other hand, focus on tidal flows in estuaries and river mouths, employing underwater turbines to generate power from tidal currents. Other systems may include hybrid approaches or emerging technologies not confined to traditional coastal or river environments, expanding the potential for tidal energy exploitation.</p></p>
