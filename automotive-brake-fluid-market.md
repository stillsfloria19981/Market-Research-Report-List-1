<p><p>The automotive brake fluid market includes two primary types: mineral oil and synthetic. Mineral oil brake fluids are derived from petroleum and are known for their stability and compatibility with certain rubber components. They are less hygroscopic, meaning they absorb less water, which can enhance longevity. Synthetic brake fluids, often glycol-based, offer superior performance in extreme temperatures and are widely used in modern vehicles. They provide higher boiling points and better overall braking efficiency, making them suitable for demanding driving conditions.</p></p>
