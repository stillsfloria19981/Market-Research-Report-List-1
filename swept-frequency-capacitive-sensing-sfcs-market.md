<p><p>The Swept Frequency Capacitive Sensing (SFCS) market encompasses various sensing types. Surface capacitance focuses on detecting changes in capacitance at the surface of objects, while projected capacitance measures the capacitance in a projected area, often used for touch applications. Self-capacitance involves measuring the capacitance of a single sensor node to detect proximity or touch, whereas mutual capacitance detects variations between multiple electrodes, allowing for more complex interactions. Each type serves distinct applications in consumer electronics, automotive, and industrial sectors.</p></p>
