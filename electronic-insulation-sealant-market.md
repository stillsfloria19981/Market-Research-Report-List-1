<p><p>The electronic insulation sealant market comprises primarily two types: organic gum and inorganic glue. Organic gums, typically derived from natural materials, provide excellent flexibility and moisture resistance, making them ideal for applications in electronics where movement and environmental factors are a concern. In contrast, inorganic glues, often based on silicates or epoxy resins, offer superior heat resistance and durability, making them suitable for high-temperature applications. Together, these sealants ensure efficient insulation and protection of electronic components in various settings.</p></p>
