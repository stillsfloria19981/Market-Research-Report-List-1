<p><p>The Bone Anchored Hearing Aids (BAHA) market consists of two main types: implantable and non-implantable devices. Implantable BAHA systems involve surgical placement of a titanium implant in the skull bone, which transmits sound vibrations directly to the inner ear, offering improved sound quality and speech recognition. Non-implantable BAHA devices, on the other hand, utilize headbands or adhesive mounts to hold the hearing aid in place without surgery, providing a less invasive option for individuals with conductive hearing loss or single-sided deafness.</p></p>
