<p><p>The artificial fiber market comprises two primary types: filament fibers and staple fibers. Filament fibers are continuous strands, often used in textiles for their smoothness and strength, ideal for fabrics like silk-like materials and industrial applications. Staple fibers are short, discrete lengths, resembling natural fibers, commonly used in spun yarns for clothing, upholstery, and non-woven products. Both types are derived from synthetic or regenerated sources, catering to diverse industries, with filament fibers excelling in durability and staple fibers offering versatility and texture.</p></p>
