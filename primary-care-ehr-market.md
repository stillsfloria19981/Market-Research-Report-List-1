<p><p>The Primary Care EHR market is divided into two main types: cloud-based and on-premise solutions. Cloud-based EHRs are hosted remotely, offering flexibility, scalability, and lower upfront costs, as users access the system via the internet. This model simplifies updates and maintenance. Conversely, on-premise EHRs are installed locally on a practice's servers, providing more control over data security and customization but requiring higher initial investments and ongoing IT management. Each option caters to different operational needs and preferences in primary care settings.</p></p>
