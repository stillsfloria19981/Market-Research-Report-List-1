<p><p>The light vehicle wheel market consists of three primary types: casting, forging, and other methods. Casting involves pouring molten metal into a mold, resulting in intricate designs and cost-effectiveness. Forging, on the other hand, involves shaping metal using compressive forces, yielding stronger and more durable wheels. Other methods include rolled, stamped, or composite wheels, which combine various materials for performance and weight optimization. Each type caters to different performance needs, aesthetics, and market segments in the automotive industry.</p></p>
