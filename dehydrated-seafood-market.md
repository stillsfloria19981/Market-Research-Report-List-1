<p><p>The dehydrated seafood market includes several drying methods, each offering unique characteristics. Spray drying involves atomizing seafood into a fine mist and drying it quickly, preserving flavor. Freeze drying removes moisture through sublimation, maintaining texture and nutrients. Vacuum drying utilizes low pressure to evaporate water at lower temperatures, ensuring quality. Sun drying relies on natural sunlight, while hot air drying circulates heated air for moisture removal. Other methods may include osmotic dehydration or microwave drying, catering to diverse consumer preferences.</p></p>
